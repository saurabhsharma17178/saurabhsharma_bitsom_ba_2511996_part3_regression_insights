# Final Recommendation

## Executive Summary

Based on the regression analysis, customer footfall, inventory availability, and marketing spend are the key factors associated with monthly sales. The multiple regression model was selected as the final model because it evaluates several business drivers together and provides more practical insights for decision-making.

---

## 1. Which factors appear most strongly associated with monthly sales?

The analysis shows that the following variables have the strongest association with monthly sales:

- Footfall (strongest positive relationship)
- Inventory Availability
- Marketing Spend

Footfall had the strongest statistical relationship with monthly sales and was highly significant in both the simple and multiple regression models.

---

## 2. Which variables should leadership focus on?

Leadership should prioritize:

- Increasing customer footfall through marketing campaigns and customer engagement.
- Maintaining high inventory availability to avoid lost sales.
- Optimizing marketing spend to improve store performance.

These variables showed statistically significant relationships with monthly sales and are practical areas for business improvement.

---

## 3. Which variables should not be over-interpreted?

The Region_N dummy variable should not be over-interpreted because it produced a numerical issue (#NUM!) in the regression output and did not provide a meaningful coefficient.

Business decisions should not rely solely on this variable without additional analysis.

---

## 4. What business action would you recommend?

Recommended actions:

- Increase customer traffic through targeted marketing and promotional campaigns.
- Improve inventory management to ensure products remain available.
- Monitor marketing effectiveness and allocate budgets to campaigns that generate higher customer visits.
- Continue collecting additional business data to improve future prediction models.

---

## 5. What risks or limitations should leadership keep in mind?

- The model explains only part of the variation in monthly sales (R² ≈ 0.458).
- Other important factors such as seasonality, local competition, promotions, pricing strategies, and economic conditions were not included.
- Regression results depend on the available historical data.

---

## 6. Why does regression show association but not automatically prove causation?

Regression identifies statistical relationships between variables but does not prove that one variable directly causes another.

For example, higher footfall is associated with higher sales, but regression alone cannot confirm that increasing footfall will always cause sales to increase. Other business factors may also influence both variables.

Business decisions should combine regression findings with business knowledge, operational experience, and additional testing.

---

# Final Recommendation

The multiple regression model is recommended for business decision-making because it evaluates multiple business drivers simultaneously.

Leadership should focus primarily on increasing customer footfall, maintaining strong inventory availability, and optimizing marketing investments to improve monthly sales while continuing to monitor additional business factors not included in the current model.
